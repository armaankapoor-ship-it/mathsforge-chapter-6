Deep notes

Section-wise notes with CBSE and JEE lenses.

Each card gives the idea, board writing angle, exam focus, and common trap.

01Rate

Rate of change

dy/dx tells how fast y changes with respect to x.

  • CBSE writing: Identify dependent and independent variables.
  • JEE focus: Related rates require chain rule.
  • Common trap: Units matter in rate questions.
02Tangent

Tangent equation

At x=a, tangent slope is f prime(a), and line is y-y1=m(x-x1).

  • CBSE writing: Find point and slope separately.
  • JEE focus: Tangent conditions often lead to parameter equations.
  • Common trap: Do not use f(a) as slope.
03Normal

Normal equation

Normal slope is -1/m when tangent slope m is non-zero.

  • CBSE writing: Use perpendicular slope relation.
  • JEE focus: Handle horizontal tangent separately.
  • Common trap: If tangent slope is 0, normal is vertical.
04Monotonicity

Increasing and decreasing

If f prime(x)>0 on an interval, function is increasing; if f prime(x)<0, it is decreasing.

  • CBSE writing: Make sign table.
  • JEE focus: Sign chart gives interval answers quickly.
  • Common trap: Check intervals, not just points.
05Critical

Critical points

Candidates for extrema occur where f prime(x)=0 or derivative is undefined.

  • CBSE writing: List critical points inside domain.
  • JEE focus: Boundary points also matter for closed intervals.
  • Common trap: f prime=0 is necessary but not always sufficient.
06MaxMin

First derivative test

If derivative changes from positive to negative, local maximum; negative to positive, local minimum.

  • CBSE writing: Show sign change around critical point.
  • JEE focus: Useful for polynomial and rational functions.
  • Common trap: No sign change means no extremum.
07Approx

Approximation by differentials

Small change dy is approximately f prime(x) dx.

  • CBSE writing: Use Delta y nearly dy for small dx.
  • JEE focus: Approximation questions rely on linearization.
  • Common trap: Approximation is not exact unless function is linear.
08Optimization

Optimization workflow

Define variable, write target function, apply derivative, test critical point.

  • CBSE writing: End with units and final sentence.
  • JEE focus: Domain restrictions are key.
  • Common trap: Do not optimize before reducing to one variable.

Formula sheet

High-yield formulas with exact usage conditions.

Use these as quick revision cards before solving examples.

Rate

Instantaneous rate

Rate of change of y w.r.t. x = dy/dx

Derivative is rate.

  • Example: If s=t^2, velocity ds/dt=2t.
  • Trap: Use correct variable.
Tangent

Tangent line

y-y1 = f prime(a)(x-a)

Slope of tangent is derivative at point.

  • Example: For y=x^2 at x=2, slope=4.
  • Trap: Point is (a,f(a)).
Normal

Normal line

m_normal = -1/m_tangent

Normal is perpendicular to tangent.

  • Example: If tangent slope 3, normal slope -1/3.
  • Trap: Fails when tangent slope is 0.
Increasing

Increasing test

f prime(x) > 0 on I => f increasing on I

Positive derivative means rising graph.

  • Example: x^2 increasing on (0,infinity).
  • Trap: Statement is interval based.
Decreasing

Decreasing test

f prime(x) < 0 on I => f decreasing on I

Negative derivative means falling graph.

  • Example: x^2 decreasing on (-infinity,0).
  • Trap: Check entire interval.
MaxMin

First derivative test

+ to - gives local max, - to + gives local min

Sign change decides nature.

  • Example: For -x^2, derivative changes + to - at 0.
  • Trap: No sign change means no extremum.
Approx

Differential approximation

Delta y approximately dy = f prime(x) Delta x

Linear estimate for small change.

  • Example: sqrt(101) approx 10 + 1/20.
  • Trap: Only for small changes.
Closed

Absolute extrema on closed interval

Check critical points and endpoints

Largest and smallest values among candidates.

  • Example: Evaluate f at endpoints and f prime=0 points.
  • Trap: Do not ignore endpoints.

Derivation bank

Proofs that can be reproduced in exams.

Open a proof, follow the steps, and check the final result.

Equation of tangent
  1. Let point be (a,f(a)).
  2. Slope of tangent is f prime(a).
  3. Line with slope m through point is y-y1=m(x-x1).
  4. Substitute m=f prime(a) and y1=f(a).

Final: y-y1=m(x-x1)

Shortcut: Point-slope form.

Warning: m is derivative at the point.

Equation of normal
  1. Tangent slope at x=a is f prime(a).
  2. Normal is perpendicular to tangent.
  3. Product of slopes is -1 when both are finite.
  4. So normal slope is -1/f prime(a).

Final: normal slope = -1/f prime(a)

Shortcut: Use perpendicular slopes.

Warning: If tangent slope is zero, normal is vertical.

First derivative test logic
  1. If f prime is positive, f increases.
  2. If f prime is negative, f decreases.
  3. Increasing before and decreasing after gives a peak.
  4. Decreasing before and increasing after gives a valley.

Final: + to - gives maximum, - to + gives minimum.

Shortcut: Read graph direction.

Warning: Check sign on intervals.

Differential approximation
  1. For small change dx, dy=f prime(x)dx.
  2. The graph near x is close to its tangent line.
  3. So actual change Delta y is approximately dy.
  4. Substitute values for quick estimate.

Final: Delta y approximately f prime(x) Delta x.

Shortcut: Use tangent line as local model.

Warning: Works best for small Delta x.

Practice bank

Exam-style questions with answer reveal.

Filter by keyword, try the question first, then reveal the answer and trap.

RateEasy

1. If s=t^2, find velocity at t=3.

Answer: 6

Velocity ds/dt=2t, so at t=3 it is 6.

Trap: Differentiate before substituting.

TangentMedium

2. Find tangent slope to y=x^2 at x=2.

Answer: 4

dy/dx=2x, so slope=4.

Trap: f(2)=4 is not automatically the slope for all functions.

NormalMedium

3. If tangent slope is 5, what is normal slope?

Answer: -1/5

Normal slope is negative reciprocal.

Trap: Do not use +1/5.

IncreasingMedium

4. Where is f(x)=x^2 increasing?

Answer: (0,infinity)

f prime(x)=2x>0 for x>0.

Trap: At x=0 derivative is zero, interval statement uses x>0.

DecreasingMedium

5. Where is f(x)=x^2 decreasing?

Answer: (-infinity,0)

2x<0 for x<0.

Trap: Do not say all real numbers.

MaxMinMedium

6. Find local maximum of f(x)=-x^2+4x+1.

Answer: 5 at x=2

f prime=-2x+4=0 gives x=2; f(2)=5 and derivative changes + to -.

Trap: Give both point/value if asked.

ClosedHard

7. For f(x)=x^2 on [-1,2], find absolute maximum.

Answer: 4 at x=2

Check x=0 and endpoints. Values: 1,0,4.

Trap: Endpoints matter.

ApproxMedium

8. Approximate sqrt(101).

Answer: 10.05

Use f(x)=sqrt x at x=100, dx=1, dy=1/(2*10)=0.05.

Trap: Do not use large dx logic.

OptimizationHard

9. A rectangle has perimeter 20. Maximize area.

Answer: 25

Let sides x and 10-x. Area x(10-x), maximum at x=5, area=25.

Trap: Use semi-perimeter correctly.

TangentMedium

10. Find tangent to y=x^2 at x=1.

Answer: y=2x-1

Point (1,1), slope 2, so y-1=2(x-1).

Trap: Use point-slope form.

NormalHard

11. Find normal to y=x^2 at x=1.

Answer: y-1=-(1/2)(x-1)

Tangent slope 2, normal slope -1/2.

Trap: Normal slope is reciprocal negative.

ProofHard

12. How do you identify local maximum using derivative sign?

Answer: f prime changes from positive to negative

Increasing before the point and decreasing after creates a peak.

Trap: f prime=0 alone is not enough.

Core formulas

  • Instantaneous rate: Rate of change of y w.r.t. x = dy/dx
  • Tangent line: y-y1 = f prime(a)(x-a)
  • Normal line: m_normal = -1/m_tangent
  • Increasing test: f prime(x) > 0 on I => f increasing on I
  • Decreasing test: f prime(x) < 0 on I => f decreasing on I
  • First derivative test: + to - gives local max, - to + gives local min

Common traps

  • Derivative sign must be checked on intervals.
  • Tangent slope is derivative, not function value.
  • Normal slope may be vertical when tangent is horizontal.
  • Critical points and endpoints both matter on closed intervals.
  • Maximum/minimum needs nature test.
  • Approximation works only for small change.
